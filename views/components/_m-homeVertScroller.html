Integration Highlights

  • 1950’s

  • Brown v. Board of Education

    May 17, 1954

    U.S. Supreme Court delivers its decision in Brown v. Board of Education of Topeka, Kansas, eliminating the “separate but equal” policy in American public schools.

  • 1960’s

  • Football

    1962

    Dick Leftridge becomes the first first African-American to play football at WVU.

  • First African-American athlete at WVU

    1961

    Phillip Edwards was the first African-American athlete at WVU, participating in men's track

  • Football

    1962

    Dick Leftridge and Roger Alford become the first African-American players to receive football scholarships at WVU.

  • Men’s basketball

    1964

    Jim Lewis, Ron Williams, Ed Harvard, and Norman Holmes make WVU sports history as the first four African-American players listed on the freshman basketball team.

  • Men’s soccer

    1965

    George Woods, Jr. became the first African American athlete to play men’s soccer at WVU.

  • Wrestling

    1965

    Norman Hill was the first African American athlete to wrestle at WVU

  • 1970’s

  • Football

    1970

    First African-American men’s assistant coach, Garrett Ford.

  • Football

    1971

    Jim Braxton was the blocking weapon for star running back O. J. Simpson. As Simpson remarks, most of his 11,236 career rushing yards were due to Braxton.

  • Title IX Passed

    June 23, 1972

    Title IX of the U.S. Education Amendments is passed, prohibiting discrimination based on sex in educational institutions.

  • Football

    1973

    First African-American starting quarterback, Ben Williams.

  • Basketball

    1974

    First African-American men’s basketball graduate assistant coach, Jackie Joe Robinson.

  • Basketball

    1974

    First all African-American starting five in men’s basketball – Levi Phillips, Eartha Faust, Warren Baker, Jerome Anderson and Larry Carr.

  • Baseball

    1976

    Bruce Clinton was offered a scholarship to become WVU’s first African-American baseball player.

  • Athletic Administrator

     1977

    First African-American athletic administrator, Garrett Ford.

  • Women’s Track

    1976

    First African-American female athlete – Cheryl Nabors, women’s track.

  • Men’s Tennis

     June 23, 1977

    Harry Moore was the first African-American athlete to participate in men’s tennis at WVU.

  • 1980’s

  • Women's Basketball

    December 21, 1984

    Georgeann Wells became the first woman to register a dunk in an official NCAA intercollegiate basketball game. She did it with a men’s regulation-size.

  • Women's tennis

    1981

    Shirley Robinson became the first African-American female athlete at WVU, participating in women’s tennis.

  • Men's Basketball

    1986

    First African-American men’s basketball assistant coach, Ron Brown.

  • Olympic Sports

    1985

    First African-American Olympic sports assistant coach, Nate Carr.

  • Gymnastics

    1987

    Yvette Clark was the first African-American athlete to participate in women’s gymnastics at WVU.

  • 1990’s

  • Women’s Basketball

    1993

    First African-American women’s basketball assistant coach, Regina Woods.

  • 2000’s

  • Soccer

    2006

    Marlon LeBlanc is hired as the head coach of men’s soccer at WVU, becoming the first African-American to be the head coach of a WVU sports team.

  • Women's Basketball

    2006

    First all African-American starting five in women’s basketball – Ashley Powell, Britney Davis-White, LaQuita Owens, Chakia Cole and Olayinka Sanni.
